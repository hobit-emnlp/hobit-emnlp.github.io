hoBIT: A Profile-Aware Retrieval-Augmented Chatbot for University Academic Advising

Identical advising questions need different curriculum evidence depending on the student profile.

Paper Code Explore

hoBIT acquires a compact session profile by dual-asking, then uses it as both a soft query prefix and a hard metadata filter.

Why hoBIT?

Academic advising is profile-dependent retrieval: semantic similarity alone is not enough.

Profile-blind RAG misses context

Graduation, course, and major rules vary by department, admission cohort, and major type, so the same query can require different documents.

Profile metadata is indexed offline

Chunks are tagged with department, admission cohort, and required profile fields before retrieval.

Dual-asking keeps interaction light

The bot asks for missing fields only when they are needed, then reuses them inside the session.

Same Question, Different Correct Answer

Like realistic search snippets, curriculum documents can look near-identical. hoBIT exposes the hidden profile signal that decides which evidence is correct.

Profile-Blind RAG

"Can you tell me my graduation requirements?"

Semantically similar document

Wrong cohort evidence, wrong answer, or insufficient user information.

hoBIT Profile-Aware RAG

Dept: CS, Cohort: 2020; graduation requirements?

Profile-matched source evidence

Correct cohort evidence, source-tracked answer, reusable session profile.

Methodology

hoBIT combines intent routing and profile-aware RAG, with profile signals introduced before indexing and again at retrieval time.

1

Intent Classification and RAG Routing

hoBIT first decides whether a message should be handled directly or routed into retrieval. Greeting, FAQ, ability, and small-talk intents avoid unnecessary generation; retrieval intents enter the profile-aware RAG pipeline.

greetingFAQabilitysmall talkretrieval
Only retrieval queries need source-grounded evidence and profile-conditioned search.
2

Profile-Based Preprocessing

Static and dynamic school documents are chunked, tagged with profile metadata, and indexed before deployment. The tags let retrieval distinguish near-identical curriculum documents from different departments, admission cohorts, and major types.

PDFschunksprofile tagsQdrant
Tags include department, admission_year, and required_profile fields used by reactive dual-asking.
3

Profile Injection at Retrieval Time

At runtime, hoBIT acquires missing profile fields through dual-asking and injects the session profile in two places: as a soft prefix that steers ranking, and as a hard payload filter that removes wrong-profile evidence.

Dept: CSCohort: 2020Major type: Intensive
The soft prefix improves recall, while the hard filter controls eligibility.

hoBIT System Overview

Offline profile-based data preprocessing meets online profile-based RAG with dual-asking, soft prefix injection, and hard payload filtering.

Runtime Pipeline: Four Stages

Intent classification, filter extraction, profile validation, and source-tracked answer generation run around a session profile.

Supported Interaction Types

hoBIT covers casual conversation, campus FAQ, school-life retrieval, and profile-adaptive academic advising in one student-facing interface. The examples below keep English query-answer pairs while faithfully translating captured hoBIT service outputs.

The undergraduate administration office of the College of Informatics is located in Room 102 of the Aegineung Student Center on Korea University's Seoul Campus. Undergraduate inquiries are generally directed to 02-3290-4132 or 02-3290-4133.

Small Talk

Intent: small talk

School Life

Can you recommend student clubs in the College of Informatics?

You can explore College of Informatics student activities based on your interests. For example, if you are interested in web or service development, KWEB may be relevant; if you are interested in security, information-security groups may fit; and if you are interested in AI or data analysis, machine learning and data science study groups are good options.

For campus-life questions, hoBIT searches both static campus-life documents and dynamic notice documents, then summarizes interest-specific candidates as a source-tracked answer.

Retrieval: school life

Latest Notices

Tell me recent announcements with sources.

Recent College of Informatics notices should be checked together with their titles, posting dates, deadlines, and attached files. hoBIT answers while preserving links and sources from multiple notice documents.

For example, scholarship or recruitment notices, special lecture announcements, and academic schedule notices may be retrieved together, and the original link for each notice is shown as a source card.

Dynamic retrieval: notices

Academic Schedule

When is the tuition payment period?

The tuition payment period is finalized according to each semester's academic calendar and finance office notices. It is usually divided into regular, additional, and final payment periods, and students should also check when tuition bills can be printed as well as the payment bank or virtual account information.

For tuition-payment questions, hoBIT classifies the query as an academic question and generates a source-tracked answer covering the payment period, tuition bill, and payment method. Before paying, students should confirm the portal tuition bill and academic calendar notice.

Retrieval: academic

Profile-Based Advising

Tell me my required major courses.

This answer is based on the entered profile: Department: Computer Science; Admission year: 2020.

For Computer Science students admitted in 2020, the 2019-2020 curriculum of the College of Informatics requires at least 130 total credits.

The credit requirements are as follows:
- General education: at least 31 credits
- Basic major: 18 credits of required major courses and at least 24 credits of major electives
- Six required basic-major courses: Data Structures, Algorithms, Computer Architecture, Operating Systems, Computer Networks, and Databases
- Intensive major: at least 30 credits of major electives
- General electives: at least 27 credits

Common graduation requirements must also be satisfied:
- Completion of a second major is required, except for transfer students entering through bachelor's admission
- Chinese character certificate is not required
- Official English score: one of TOEIC 650, TOEFL PBT 530/CBT 197/IBT 74, TOSEL(A) 512, TEPS 571, or IELTS 5.5
- Completion of three English-taught courses

Because major credit requirements may differ depending on double major, interdisciplinary major, or transfer status, final graduation eligibility should be confirmed through the portal graduation-credit page and department administration guidance.

Results

Profile injection dominates the retrieval stack and makes profile-blind boosters redundant.

Source Match 0.070 -> 0.585

Adding profile injection produces the largest jump in the deployed ablation.

Overall PASS 6.8% -> 47.4%

Dual-asking plus profile-aware retrieval makes correct answers reachable.

Intent routing 96.0% acc.

The retrieval-intent gate reaches 0.990 F1, so only retrieval queries enter the profile-aware pipeline.

Open-ended advising 0.796 overall

The ground-truth-free track reaches 59.1% Overall PASS with the deployed pipeline.
